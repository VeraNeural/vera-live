# VERA Acceptable Use Policy

> **Document ID:** AUP-001  
> **Version:** 1.0  
> **Effective Date:** January 25, 2026  
> **Classification:** Internal  

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines acceptable use of VERA company systems, networks, devices, and data. It establishes expectations for responsible use and outlines prohibited activities to protect the organization, its users, and its data.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and third-party personnel
- All company-owned and personal devices used for work
- All company systems, applications, and services
- All data accessed, processed, or stored for business purposes

---

## 3. Acceptable Use

### 3.1 General Principles

Personnel may use company systems for:
- Business operations and job-related activities
- Professional development and training
- Limited personal use that does not interfere with work duties
- Testing and development within authorized environments

### 3.2 Email and Communications

**Acceptable:**
- Business communications with customers, vendors, and colleagues
- Professional networking and industry communications
- Sharing work-related information with authorized recipients

**Requirements:**
- Use company email for business communications
- Include appropriate confidentiality notices
- Verify recipient addresses before sending sensitive information
- Report suspicious emails immediately

### 3.3 Internet and Cloud Services

**Acceptable:**
- Research and reference for job duties
- Authorized cloud services (GitHub, Slack, etc.)
- Professional development resources
- Business-related social media

**Requirements:**
- Use only approved cloud services for company data
- Do not store confidential data in unauthorized services
- Follow secure browsing practices
- Report suspicious websites or downloads

### 3.4 Development and Code

**Acceptable:**
- Writing and reviewing code for company projects
- Using approved development tools and environments
- Contributing to authorized open-source projects
- Testing in designated environments

**Requirements:**
- Follow secure coding practices
- Never commit secrets or credentials to repositories
- Use code review for all changes
- Follow change management procedures

---

## 4. Prohibited Activities

### 4.1 Strictly Prohibited

The following activities are **strictly prohibited** and may result in immediate termination:

| Category | Prohibited Activity |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Security** | Attempting to bypass security controls or authentication |
| **Security** | Sharing credentials or authentication tokens |
| **Security** | Installing unauthorized software or malware |
| **Security** | Accessing systems or data without authorization |
| **Data** | Copying confidential data to unauthorized locations |
| **Data** | Sharing user data externally without authorization |
| **Data** | Selling or misusing customer information |
| **Data** | Failing to report data breaches |
| **Conduct** | Harassment, discrimination, or hostile communications |
| **Conduct** | Accessing or distributing illegal content |
| **Conduct** | Using systems for personal financial gain |
| **Conduct** | Representing personal views as company positions |

### 4.2 Prohibited Without Authorization

The following require explicit written authorization:

- Penetration testing or vulnerability scanning
- Connecting external devices to company networks
- Sharing company information with media or press
- Installing software not on approved list
- Modifying system configurations
- Accessing another user's accounts or data
- Using company resources for external projects

### 4.3 Social Media Guidelines

**Prohibited:**
- Sharing confidential company information
- Speaking on behalf of the company without authorization
- Posting user data or conversations
- Disparaging customers, partners, or colleagues
- Sharing internal discussions or decisions

**Required:**
- Clearly state personal opinions are your own
- Maintain professional conduct
- Report suspicious contact attempts

---

## 5. Data Handling

### 5.1 Classification Compliance

All personnel must:
- Handle data according to its classification level
- Not downgrade data classification without authorization
- Use approved tools for each classification level
- Report misclassified data

### 5.2 Confidential Data

When handling confidential or restricted data:
- Access only what is needed for job duties
- Do not copy to personal devices
- Use encryption when transmitting
- Securely delete when no longer needed
- Do not discuss in public spaces

### 5.3 User Data

VERA user data requires special handling:
- Never access user conversations without authorization
- Never share user data externally
- Use only anonymized data for testing
- Report any potential data exposure immediately
- Follow GDPR/CCPA requirements

---

## 6. Device and System Security

### 6.1 Company Devices

Personnel using company devices must:
- Enable full-disk encryption
- Use strong passwords/biometrics
- Enable automatic screen lock (5 minutes)
- Keep systems updated
- Report lost or stolen devices immediately
- Not modify security settings

### 6.2 Personal Devices (BYOD)

Personal devices used for work must:
- Meet minimum security requirements
- Have separate work and personal profiles where possible
- Not store confidential data locally
- Be reported if lost or stolen
- Be available for remote wipe of company data

### 6.3 Remote Work

Personnel working remotely must:
- Use secure, private networks
- Avoid public WiFi for sensitive work
- Ensure physical security of devices
- Not allow unauthorized persons to view screens
- Follow home office security guidelines

---

## 7. Monitoring and Privacy

### 7.1 Monitoring Notice

VERA reserves the right to monitor:
- Email and communications on company systems
- Internet usage and browsing history
- File access and data transfers
- System and application logs
- Security events and anomalies

**Purpose:** Monitoring is conducted for security, compliance, and operational purposes only.

### 7.2 No Expectation of Privacy

Personnel should have no expectation of privacy when using:
- Company email and messaging systems
- Company devices and networks
- Company applications and services
- Data stored on company systems

### 7.3 Personal Information

VERA will:
- Minimize collection of personal information
- Use monitoring data only for stated purposes
- Protect monitoring data appropriately
- Comply with applicable privacy laws

---

## 8. Incident Reporting

### 8.1 What to Report

Personnel must immediately report:
- Security incidents or suspected breaches
- Lost or stolen devices
- Phishing attempts or suspicious emails
- Unauthorized access attempts
- Policy violations observed
- Software vulnerabilities discovered
- Unusual system behavior

### 8.2 How to Report

| Urgency | Method |
|---------|--------|
| Critical (active breach) | Phone call to Security Officer + email |
| High (potential breach) | Email to security@veraneural.com immediately |
| Medium (suspicious activity) | Email within 4 hours |
| Low (policy questions) | Normal business channels |

### 8.3 Non-Retaliation

VERA prohibits retaliation against anyone who:
- Reports security incidents in good faith
- Reports policy violations
- Participates in investigations
- Raises security concerns

---

## 9. Enforcement

### 9.1 Violation Consequences

| Severity | Examples | Consequences |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| Minor | Unintentional policy deviation | Coaching, training |
| Moderate | Repeated minor violations | Written warning |
| Serious | Intentional policy violation | Suspension, termination |
| Severe | Data breach, illegal activity | Immediate termination, legal action |

### 9.2 Investigation Process

1. Incident reported or detected
2. Initial assessment by Security Officer
3. Evidence preservation
4. Investigation conducted
5. Findings documented
6. Appropriate action taken
7. Lessons learned captured

---

## 10. Training and Acknowledgment

### 10.1 Training Requirements

All personnel must complete:
- Acceptable Use Policy training at onboarding
- Annual policy refresher training
- Additional training as policies change

### 10.2 Acknowledgment

All personnel must acknowledge:
- Reading and understanding this policy
- Agreement to comply with policy requirements
- Understanding of consequences for violations

---

## 11. Policy Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy:
- Must be requested in writing
- Require Security Officer approval
- Are time-limited (maximum 1 year)
- Include compensating controls
- Are documented and tracked
